Krishna's Gita
 

Welcome to this conversation! » 

The Gita is a conversation between Lord Krishna and Arjuna. When Arjuna developed cold feet on the battlefield, Krishna revealed the fundamental truth of life and living to him. This conversation between Krishna and Arjuna is also a journey through our selves, our purpose and our relation with the Lord.
Part of the epic poem Mahabharata, composed by [vEdavyAsa], this conversation is narrated by Sanjaya to the blind king [dhr'tarAShTra]. The poetry is rich in meaning, yielding multiple possible interpretations. It requires some explanation to grasp with consistency. Several commentaries have been written and they help us interpret this work.
Ramanuja made a consistent, interwoven commentary along the philosophy of [vishiShTAdvaita]. This philosophy has one Supreme Lord, having the material world and all conscious beings as its attributes. Krishna is the Supreme Lord.
Ramanuja's commentary on the Gita dates back a thousand years.

Do we need to believe in the Lord to benefit from this conversation? Well, to believe, you first need to know yourself and your relation with the Lord. That's what this conversation is about, anyway. To start, it's sufficient to allow for His presence. Let's relish the conversation and not be stuck in denial.

This is an English-translation of Ramanuja's commentary on the Gita. Transliteration of Sanskrit in the English alphabet is in square braces [ ].
About the tendency to use the masculine: The Lord is referred to as 'Him'. References to people are in the masculine gender as well. This is a limitation of language and purely for ease of expression. As you will see, the Lord and the core of our selves are subtle and not subject to limitations of gender.

Back to Basics

Who am I?

I am not my body, am I?

I am not just a thought, am I?

What am I? Let's begin the discovery of the Self and its relation with the Lord.

Yoga is a journey to discover reality. Every form of yoga leads to the same discovery.

What am I doing?

Our journey in life is a series of activities. Is it about 'doing' or 'making it happen'?

Why am I doing it?

If the action doesn't guarantee an outcome, why should I work, or give my 100% to achieve a goal?

Look beyond desire. Focus on the purpose even when you don't reach the goal you expect.

Is there a different way?

What's it like to be really free?

Chapter 1

We often doubt and worry about achieving wealth, success and other outcomes.

In our anxiety, we interpret anything that happens as a signal of doom

It doesn't matter who you are or what you're asking for. Be in this conversation. Keep enquiring.

In the midst of action, we sometimes worry 'Am I going to hurt someone? Is the outcome worth it?'

Chapter 2

Why do I hesitate?

Hesitation happens when you mix-up the Self with your body and the rest of the environment.

The Self is separate from the environment and your body. The Self is ever-lasting. So is the Lord.

Is there anything other than the observable universe?

Is the universe only an effect of the law governing it?

Let's be free of doubt in our journey to realization

We are distinct from the Lord. Each of us is distinct as well.

Is there any possibility that the distinctions are a figment of our imagination?

No, we don't need to doubt our existence in our journey of realization and worship.

Whenever you are driven by outcomes, you confuse the Self with the environment.

Tolerate your experiences. They are effects of past activities that were driven by outcomes.

Do your duties to attain liberation. Then those experiences do not drain you.

How is the Self different?

The Self is pure and unbounded knowledge. It has been that way and will always remain so.

The Self is indestructible because it is subtle. It's not made up of anything else.

The Self brings awareness - the power to experience and know.

The Self owns different bodies over time. It remains unchanged even as the body keeps changing.

We get into situations by the will of the Lord. He is always with us.

Duty is superior to hesitation and inaction - irrespective of the outcome.

Outcomes of our actions do not matter when we're aware of the Self.

When outcomes don't matter, we can't do anything wrong.

It doesn't really matter what drives your action; Do it for the Lord.

The world is full of passion and ignorance. Don't let them control you.

You have the authority to initiate activity. You are not the one who makes things happen.

Do not avoid activity.

Why know the Self?

Working with an awareness of the Self brings out the best in us - the skill in our actions.

An experience of the Self is so pleasant that likes and dislikes don't matter anymore.

It isn't possible to experience the Self without placing ourselves in the refuge of the Lord.

Chapter 3

Should I employ my organs in activity? Or pull them back?

You reach the goal by being active and offering your activity as service to the Lord.

Focusing your thought without being affected by this world is another way to achieve the goal.

You cannot exist in this world without activity.

Restraint and sacrifice are pretense when you still yearn for 'what could have been'.

Do your activities to realize your Self - instead of having your attachments agitate you.

Often, our activities themselves cause agitation, don't they?

It's true that activity needs a purpose. Make the Lord's worship as your purpose.

Whatever your purpose, whomever you work for - it's the Lord's worship and He is the enjoyer.

Everything happens for the Lord's worship

Activity happens in the environment, not for our purpose. The Self must engage in worship.

Though the Lord isn't bound to any duty or purpose, He stays active to reassure us.

We realize our Self by being active while acknowledging that we don't make things happen.

Delight in your activity. It is part of the Lord's play.

Without knowing the Self, we believe that we are our body.

If we know that the environment makes things happen, why should we involve in activity at all?

Even in the realization that the Self doesn't make things happen, be active and inspire others.

Dedicate your activity to the Lord.

Everything happens by the will of The Lord, for his pleasure alone.

Even if we don't live this all the time, it is still sufficient to simply accept His existence.

When you deny that The Lord is the basis of your existence, you live a myth.

Even when you believe in the Lord, desire and hatred are inevitable. Beware of them.

Work according to your nature. Don't be anxious about your goal. Your work will continue.

Even after knowing this, why are we ever-anxious about outcomes? Why do we feel regret and guilt?

Recognize desire as the cause of regret and guilt. It blocks your destiny. Look beyond desire.

To look beyond desire, work without being driven by outcomes.

Chapter 4

The Lord says- 'I've been teaching this method for millions of years'

Does the Lord take birth?

The Lord is supreme and there's nothing He needs. Why does he need to take birth in this world?

The Lord says- I take birth by My own will, retaining all of my characteristics

I form Myself, so that My followers can grasp and worship Me.

I am easily grasped in any form you expect.

Though the Lord is so easy to grasp, we remain focused on other short-term accomplishments.

Given that the Lord is supreme, is He the One who traps us in short-term gratification?

The Lord doesn't trap us. We are trapped by our own expectations.

Given that we're trapped by our experience, where does activity figure?

Be active without being driven by desire. Be liberated from the burden of expectations.

When we know the true nature of the Self, we realize that it isn't bound by activity. Things happen.

Activity is an inquiry into the nature of the Self.

Activity without attachment is the realization of the Self.

All activities burn something provided by the Lord, for the purpose of the Lord. This is worship.

How do I worship with activity?

Worship is to remove the pre-occupation with the material world.

Working, offering something, breathing, self-control: each one is a form of worship.

A person who doesn't recognize worship is living a myth and is doomed to fail.

In each activity, recognize the Self and its relationship with the Lord,

Awareness of the Self and the Lord liberates us from our flaws.

Doubts and suspicions come from ignorance. Get rid of it by working without attachments.

Chapter 5

Isn't it easier to realize the Self by contemplation, instead of doing all kinds of activities?

Working without being driven is the quickest way to look beyond desires

The Self in every person is identical. It doesn't make anything happen.

The illusion that we make things happen is a result of our past deeds.

Happenings and asymmetry belong in the environment.

The Self is pure knowledge, covered over by ignorance. This makes the variety of this world.

When ignorance is gone, we see all beings as being equal, equally related to the Lord.

The joy in the Self is beyond any enjoyment dictated by our preferences and aversions.

Worship the Lord to experience the joy of the Self

Chapter 6

We get driven by expectations when we think that we are our body.

Tranquility of the Self is gained by working without being driven

Driving or being driven - it's your choice

Offer your work to the Lord and be with Him constantly

Yoga is the realization of your Self and its intimacy with the Lord. It is a state of being.

The Self in you is identical to the Self in every other being

The characteristic of every Self is identical to the Lord

Is such a state of being really possible?

Your intention to drive lets you stay in the state of yoga.

What if my intention breaks?

Don't worry; your intention is never destroyed

What is our intimacy with the Lord?

We spoke about realizing the Self within us. What about its intimacy with the Lord?

Chapter 7

Worship is an uninterrupted chain of thought

Devotion is to worship with friendship

Awareness of the Lord's uniqueness generates affection towards Him

Our understanding comes from breakdown and analysis. The Lord cannot be analyzed in this way, so how do we bring Him into our thoughts?

The universe is one of The Lord's natures, which all conscious beings experience

Consciousness - the ability to know - is The Lord's primary nature

Why do we pursue constrained happiness?

It isn't possible to see beyond our cognition of this material world, unless we surrender to the Lord.

We have different objectives and we surrender to the Lord accordingly

Once we know the Lord, other objectives seem insignificant

Our true nature is to be of service to the Lord alone.

Dedication to a cause is dedication to the Lord

Your outcomes are as temporary as you intend

Most people don't recognize Krishna as the permanent enjoyer of everything

Ignorance and pre-occupation with life's dualities are part of our characteristics

While everyone is born with delusion, how do some of us end up knowing the Lord?

By leaving the misfortune of delusion, we get to know the Lord and worship him for a purpose

Chapter 8

How do we get to know the Lord?

Start by knowing the body of the Lord - The Self, the environment and its activities

Those who think of the Lord while leaving their body will attain the Lord

The final thought comes by habit. Make it a habit to think about the Lord.

If you yearn for wealth, think of the Lord as the owner of all wealth

If you yearn for timeless knowledge of your Self, think of the Lord as the core of the Self

If you yearn for the Lord Himself, just adore him!

The material universe keeps going in cycles. So does a person who yearns for material wealth

The Self is ever-lasting, so the one who yearns for the Self isn't stuck in creation and destruction

The person who adores the Lord gets the Lord Himself. He goes beyond all cycles

Chapter 9

Worship the Lord with devotion

Awareness comes out as devotion in very few people, though it's easy to practice

The will of the Lord causes all beings to 'be'

The Lord doesn't discriminate. He supervises.

Without knowing this reality, people go behind imaginary goals

Worship and take refuge in The Lord - He is everything

The Lord takes care of those who take His refuge

The Lord lovingly accepts all offerings and activities of his devotees

What can we possibly offer the Lord of the universe?

Without exception, everyone can devote themselves to the Lord

Chapter 10

Unfortunately, nobody knows the Lord

Let's get to know the Lord

The Lord gifts His awareness to those who want Him

The Lord's qualities cannot be understood. So how do we think of Him?

The specialties of the Lord are endless

You experience the Lord whenever you experience something special

The Lord is the pervasive essence that inspires

Chapter 11

When we believe in the Lord, He will show Himself and give us the ability to see Him.

This entire universe forms a small part of the Lord's body

You can imagine the Lord anyway you like

The Lord encompasses the whole world with all His activities.

At times we experience the universe as being terrible, due to the influence of past deeds.

We only need to request the Lord to step out of a terrible experience

A true devotee of the Lord isn't attached to anything other than the Lord, so doesn't experience agitation.

Chapter 12

Let's devote ourselves to the Lord

How do we realize our goal? By being devoted to it, or by knowing everything about it?

Being devoted is to trust and be with the Lord, recognizing that He is the ultimate purpose

Knowing is about deciphering ourselves and our relationship with the Lord

Deciphering the Self is tough. Entrust yourself to the Lord - He accepts you directly.

When it isn't possible to adore the Lord or decipher the Self, work without attachment

Adoring the Lord is special. Aspire to adore.

Chapter 13

Devotion in this world

The knower of the body is not the body, it is the Self.

That which knows the Self is the Lord. Doesn't that make the Self redundant? Is it same as the Lord?

The Lord is both the effect and the cause

The Self and the environment are effects of the Lord's creation

The Self and the environment have their own characteristics, while being part of the Lord's form.

The body is a collection of elements formed since the birth of the universe

When we recognize the body for what it is, we live the life we're meant to live

What obstacles come in the way of our lives?

Ignorance prevents the Self from expressing its completeness

The Self is pure knowledge

Use the body to get entangled or liberated - it's your choice

The Self has been entangled since beginning-less time

A person who distinguishes the Self gets disentangled.

Though the Self pervades the body, it doesn't have any characteristics of the body

Chapter 14

The three qualities bind the Self in a body.

Sattva is the joy of knowledge.

Rajas is passion.

Tamas is ignorance and lethargy.

At any point in time, one of these qualities dominates the other two.

The Self initiates action. Outcomes happen as a result of the qualities that bind the person.

Step-over with Awareness and Devotion

Self-awareness is an enabler to step over the three qualities.

The three qualities can only be overcome by surrendering to the Lord with devotion.

Chapter 15

The extent of your entanglement doesn't matter. Surrender to the source and get free.

The Self is a part of the Lord, the source of awareness.

Whatever the Self is aware of, that is also the Lord.

The Lord is superior to all of this.

Chapter 16

Isn't it tough to follow the Lord's instructions?

Our attitudes are an effect of deeds we committed in the past.

An attitude of disregard is a way to torture ourselves.

We can choose to stop torturing ourselves.

Chapter 17

Where does difficulty come from?

Our past gives the qualities of our body and our outlook to life.

Given an outlook, we can choose to live in reality or live in vain.

Food builds our body and the qualities in it.

A purpose of devotion brings knowledge and well-being.

Any other purpose brings restlessness.

Purposelessness brings ignorance.

The three qualities give behavior. Our behavior enhances them further.

What are the Lord's instructions?

The sound of 'Om' points to a higher purpose

The Lord, as the highest purpose, has great expanse.

That purpose exists and it always brings excellence.

Chapter 18

In this quest of purpose, what do we let go of?

Let go of outcomes and possessiveness. Don't be the reason.

Don't give up activity.

Isn't activity designed to give outcomes, which we are bound to?

If we aren't the reason for outcomes, what causes things to happen?

The action we conceive depends on our awareness.

Our intention leads to activity.

During the activity, we achieve the state-of-being as a doer.

Resolve and Enthusiasm are our state-of-being when we let-go of outcomes.

We discern with our intellect.

We sustain with our resolve.

Activities are done in the pursuit of happiness.

This is the material world - it is full of the three qualities. Nurture sattva and adore the Lord.

By worshipping the Lord with our actions, we let go.

With this worship, we gain limitless love for the Lord.

Worship with activities that are natural to you - even your material desires and pursuits.

If you aren't working for the Lord, you are at the mercy of your environment.

Surrender and let the Lord liberate you. Don't worry.

Share it with others who adore the Lord.

Listen to someone who shares.

Krishna is in each of us and He is our best friend. Everything we need will come to us.
